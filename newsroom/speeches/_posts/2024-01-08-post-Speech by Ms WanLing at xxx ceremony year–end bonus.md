<p><em>Background on "Declared Online Locations"</em></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Under Section 32 of POFMA, an online location may be declared as a “Declared Online Location” (“DOL”) if:</p><p></p><p>i. It has carried three or more different false statements of fact that are the subject of active Part 3 or Part 4 Directions (e.g., POFMA Correction Directions); and</p><p></p><p>ii. At least three of these false statements of fact had been communicated on the online location within six months before the date of the Declaration.</p><p></p></li><li><p>A notice must be published in the Government Gazette when a Declaration is issued. A registry of DOLs can be found at the POFMA Office website.</p><p></p></li><li><p>There are four other online locations that are currently DOLs with effect from 22 Jul 2023 up to 21 July 2025:</p><p>i. The Online Citizen Asia’s (TOCA’s) website</p><p>ii. TOCA’s Facebook page</p><p>iii. TOCA’s X (formerly known as Twitter) account page</p></li></ul><p></p>
